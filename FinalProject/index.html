Introduction to The Elder Scrolls Universe

The universe of the Elder Scrolls is a deep and complex one with very convoluted and profound lore. The real life chronology of the games goes back to 1994, beginning with the release of The Elder Scrolls: Arena. This was followed by the release of The Elder Scrolls II: Daggerfall two years later in 1996. The original games were a far cry from what they would become starting with and following The Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind. The first two games were first-person adventuring fantasy games that ran in MS DOS. The second game, Daggerfall is still impressive to this day however, due to the incredible size of the map and the scale of procedurally generated locations in the game. The map of Daggerfall, which includes two regions of Tamriel, High Rock and Hammerfell, is nearly the size of Great Britain. The map comprises 62,394 miles of space, for comparison, TESV: Skyrim's map only spans 15 miles. You'll find below a map of the continent of Tamriel, the setting of all of the games

Each region of Tamriel is home to one of Tamriel's ten Humanoid races. High Rock is home to the Bretons, Skyrim is home to the Nords, Elsweyr is home to the Khajiit. The Sumerset Isles are home to the High Elves or altmer, Morrowind is home to the Dunmer or dark elves, Black Marsh is home to the Argonians. Cyrodiil is home to the Imperials, Valenwood is home to the Wood elves or bosmer, Hammerfell is home to the Redguards. The Orcs don't have their own province, but they mostly dwell in Orsinium in eastern High Rock and Western Skyrim. Each of these regions and their respective races have their own unique and interesting stories. Each of these races also has their own natural advantages and disadvantages.
